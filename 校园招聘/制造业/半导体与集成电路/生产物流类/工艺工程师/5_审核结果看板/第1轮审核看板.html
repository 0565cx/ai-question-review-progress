本轮题目主要出错点总结

Round 1 question issues concentrated in three areas. First, some stems confused core semiconductor concepts, such as treating response variables as DOE factors, mixing overlay with CD, or equating lithography soft bake with PECVD pre-bake. Second, duplicate and near-duplicate stems were frequent, especially in DOE design, WAT/CP/FT test programs, PVD basics, cleanroom control, and packaging reliability troubleshooting. Third, a smaller number of stems had impossible process timing or internal contradictions, such as attributing CP-stage defects to later wire bonding or pairing a significant p-value with a confidence interval that contains zero. Future question generation should enforce process sequence, input/output variable boundaries, and cross-batch deduplication before release.

本轮答案主要出错点总结

Round 1 answer issues were dominated by B-type answer defects: incorrect technical knowledge, off-scope role framing, and answers that did not directly address the stem. Common examples included wrong standards or test names, such as HTGB versus THB/HAST and incorrect JEDEC references; non-control parameters presented as direct control variables, such as focus for overlay or bonding time for loop height; front-end process questions answered in packaging or test-engineer language; and first-layer answers that gave generic management frameworks instead of the requested mechanism or example. Regenerated answers should directly answer the stem in layer one, expand the mechanism in layer two, and keep layer three to plausible edge cases without introducing unrelated OCAP or process-management content.

后续重新出内容时应补充的规则

【重新出题】
Before regenerating questions, run same-batch and cross-batch deduplication for definition, why, three-factor troubleshooting, and causal-diagnosis templates. Each stem must distinguish controllable input factors, response variables, measurement metrics, and process stage. Standards, test names, and process sequence must be checked for WAT, CP, FT, HAST, PVD, lithography, etch, and packaging topics. Avoid creating variants that only change equipment names, material names, or numeric values while preserving the same answer path.

【重出答案】
Regenerated answers must answer the exact stem in the first layer, avoid unsupported ratios or over-specific ranges, and cite only correct standards and test names. Answers must stay within the role boundary: front-end process questions should not be answered as packaging or test operations unless the stem explicitly asks for cross-team handling. The third layer must remain consistent with the physical mechanism in the stem and must not reverse causality, break process timing, or replace the requested mechanism with a generic workflow.
