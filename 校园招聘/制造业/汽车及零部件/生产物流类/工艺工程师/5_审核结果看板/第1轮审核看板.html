本轮题目主要出错点总结

Round 1 question defects cluster around oral-answer fit, knowledge-boundary accuracy, and duplication. Some prompts ask candidates to explain calculation logic, formulas, symbols, or diagram-like content, which is not suitable for spoken interview answers. A smaller group of prompts mixes unrelated process mechanisms, material states, or standard stages into the stem. The largest issue is C2-style homogeneity: many process-abnormality, APQP/PPAP, control-plan, containment, root-cause, corrective-action, and standardization questions use different surface scenarios but lead candidates to nearly identical answer frameworks.

本轮答案主要出错点总结

Round 1 answer defects are mostly B-type issues, especially B1 and B3. Frequent patterns include incorrect professional facts or standard references for materials, APQP/PPAP, SPC, die-casting alloys, and process-flow symbols; answers that do not directly address the mechanism, judgment basis, or process node requested in the stem; and template-like management language replacing concrete process-engineering judgment. Some third-layer answers also describe abnormal or complex situations that do not fit the process engineer role or the actual manufacturing scenario.

后续重新出内容时应补充的规则

【重新出题】
When regenerating questions, lock each item to one knowledge point and one realistic process-engineering scenario. Avoid repeatedly wrapping the same abnormality-identification, containment, root-cause, corrective-action, and standardization framework in different scenarios. Do not ask candidates to write formulas, draw diagrams, derive calculations, or explain symbol meanings. For APQP, PPAP, SPC, material grades, die-casting alloys, and process-flow topics, verify terminology, stage names, version references, and applicability before finalizing the stem.

【重出答案】
When regenerating answers, each layer must answer the stem directly. Layer 1 should give accurate concepts and standards; Layer 2 should compare common role-relevant solutions and justify the choice; Layer 3 should describe realistic frequent exceptions and complex constraints. Facts about materials, heat treatment, SPC charts, APQP/PPAP stages, process-flow symbols, and alloy grades must be checked. Avoid generic actions such as investigation, review, SOP update, or training unless tied to concrete engineering criteria and scoring points.
